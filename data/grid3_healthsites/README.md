# GRID3 health facility counts by health zone

[NOTE: Before using, please read this issue on the processing of the data.](https://github.com/kraemer-lab/Ebola_DRC_2026/issues/14)

Health-zone-level counts and **density** of health facilities in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), from [GRID3 COD Health Facilities v8.0](https://data.grid3.org/datasets/GRID3::grid3-cod-health-facilities-v8-0/about), aggregated to `data/shapefiles/DRC_Health_zones.shp`.

These data support outbreak analyses where facility density, care capacity proxies, or gaps in the formal health network matter. GRID3 integrates Ministry of Health and partner sources; it is generally **more complete** than crowdsourced OSM-only layers (see `data/healthsites_io/` for comparison).

## About GRID3 COD Health Facilities v8.0

[GRID3](https://grid3.org/) maintains harmonised geospatial data for development and health planning. **COD Health Facilities v8.0** is a national point layer of health establishments in DRC (centres de santé, postes de santé, hospitals, dispensaries, and related types), compiled with the Ministère de la Santé Publique, Hygiène et Prévention and partners.

**Citation:** Center for Integrated Earth System Information (CIESIN), Columbia University; Ministère de la Santé Publique, Hygiène et Prévention, DRC; GRID3 (2025). *GRID3 COD — Health Facilities v8.0.* <https://doi.org/10.7916/f1ft-y872>

**Licence:** Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0). See `raw/COD_GRID3_health_facilities_v8_0.shp.xml` for full metadata.

## Method (this repo)

1.  **Load facilities** — Unzip `raw/COD_GRID3_health_facilities_v8_0_*.zip` and read the shapefile (\~38,000 points). **No facility-type filter** is applied (all records in the layer are counted).
2.  **Zone geometry** — `data/shapefiles/DRC_Health_zones.shp`; `st_make_valid()` on zones.
3.  **Disambiguate `nom`** — Where the same zone name appears in multiple provinces (**Bili**, **Lubunga**), `nom` is suffixed with province, e.g. `Lubunga (Tshopo)` (repo schema contract).
4.  **Spatial join** — `st_intersects`: count = number of facility points intersecting each health zone.
5.  **Density** — `healthsite_density = healthsite_count / area_km²` (`st_area`, converted to km²).
6.  **Export** — Tabular CSVs with `st_drop_geometry()`.

**Units**

| Variable | Unit | Description |
|----|----|----|
| `healthsite_count` | count | Integer number of GRID3 points in the zone |
| `healthsite_density` | sites per km² | `healthsite_count` / zone area |

| `nom` | Health-zone name (province suffix where needed for duplicates) |

`write.csv()` may add a leading `X` column (row index); ignore for analysis.

**Example (R):**

``` r
library(here)

g3 <- read.csv(here("data/grid3_healthsites/processed/grid3_healthsites__healthsite_count__static.csv"))
g3[g3$healthsite_count > 200, c("nom", "healthsite_count")]
```

## Data quality and limitations

| Issue | Detail |
|----|----|
| **Point-in-polygon** | Counts use `st_intersects`; facilities on boundaries may be assigned to one zone; no catchment modelling. |
| **All facility types** | Unlike `healthsites_io`, no exclusion of pharmacies/labs—totals reflect the full GRID3 layer. |
| **Double counting at borders** | Points are counted per zone polygon; very dense border areas are possible where polygons overlap points once each only. |
| **Duplicate `nom`** | Resolved via `nom (PROVINCE)` in export; use `ZSCode` from the shapefile for strict joins. |
| **Coverage** | \~38,049 national points; **1 zone** has zero facilities in this extract; zone totals sum to \~37,997 (52 points may fall outside health-zone polygons or on boundaries). |
| **Static snapshot** | v8.0 release; not a time series in this folder. |
